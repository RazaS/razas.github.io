title: Active Multiple Myeloma
updated: 2022-01-25 16:09:54Z
created: 2021-07-12 23:16:33Z

Definition 

  • Neoplastic, clonal proliferation of plasma cells producing monoclonal immunoglobulin (usually M protein) resulting in endprognos-organ dysfunction
  • Usually mono-clonal and secretory

Epidemiology
**
**

  • Affects older people, median age 68y, M > F, blacks > whites
  • 2% of all cancers in US
  • Half survive at 5 years

Variant

Non-secretory Myeloma

Esp. IgD and IgE

Plasma Cell Leukemia

Pathophysiology
**
**

  • Malignant plasma cell line secretes monoclonal antibody

  • 95% produce M protein (monoclogal Ig = identical heavy chain + identical light chain; OR light chain only)

    • IgG 50%, IgA 20%, IgD 2%, IgM 0.5%)

    • 15-20% produce free light chains only

    • <5% secrete nothing

      • Oligosecretory

  • Light chains

    • Found in Serum as Kappa or Lambda light chain. Kappa > lamda has better prognosis
    • Found in Urine as Bence-Jones protein

CKD and K/L

  • The rapid turnover of FLC (2-6 hours) as compared with IgG (23 days) and other immunoglobulins (ie, Ig: 5.8 days; IgM: 5.1 days; IgD: 2.8 days; IgE: 2.3 days) contribute to FLC lability and thus make it a useful disease burden monitoring tool
  • Normally, K / L production ratio is 2:1.  K/L ratio is < 1 because kappa is a monomer and cleared more readily by kidneys. Under CKD, K/L ratio is >1 because kappa chains cannot be cleared, and both are cleared by the reticuloendothelial system

Clinical Features and Complications (SLIM CRAB HANd)
...

*
**
amyloidosis, if actually attributable to myeloma, makes you myeloma too!
**
**

  • S stands for 60%: those patients who have 60% or more clonal plasma cells in the bone marrow were so likely to progress that they ought to be treated and are now classified as symptomatic myeloma

  • Li stands for light chains: those patients with a kappa-to-lambda or lambda-to-kappa ratio of greater than 100 are so likely to progress to needing treatment that they should be treated at the time of diagnosis

  • M stands for MRI: patients with more than one focal lesion on MRI now have transitioned from smoldering myeloma to symptomatic disease warranting therapy

  • HyperCalcemia

    • Bones, groans, moans, stones, psychiatric overtones
    • Polyuria, polydipsia

  • Renal disease

    • Cast nephropathy, particularly from light chain disease
    • BUN to Cr ratio of <20:1 (intinsic injury to kidney)
    • Immediate treatment: proteasome inhibitor salvages the kidney
    • Urine sediment should be bland (nephrotic range proteinuria with MGS is uncommon; you need to have a renal biopsy at that point)

  • Anemia

    • Normocytic anemia
    • Weakness, fatigue, pallor
    • From bone marrow suppression
    • Rouleaux formation

  • Bone Disease (70%)

    • Pain (back)
    • Pathological fractures
    • Lytic lesions (skull, spine, ribs, proximal long bones)
    • Increased bone resorption secondary to PTHrP
    • Due to osteoclast activating factor
    • MIRELS Score

  • Weight loss

  • Infections

    • S. Pneumo and Gram -, secondary to plasma cell dysfunction

  • Bleeding

    • thrombocytopenia
    • acquired vWD

  • Extra-medullary plasmacytoma

    • Soft tissue mass composed o fmonoclonal plasma cells

  • Hyperviscosity 

    • Strokes, headache, angina, MI

    • M protein hyperviscosity

Amyloidosis
...

- Accumulation of insoluble fibrillar protein (Ig light chain in tissues)
- Cardiac (HFpEF, arrythmias, syncope)
- GI (malabsorption, large tongue)
- Neurologic (orthostatic hypotension, carpal tunnel)
- Factor 10 deficiency (racoon eyes)
  • Neurological
    • Muscle weakness, pain, parathesia
    • Spinal cord compression
    • Nerve entrapment from bone collapse 

Revised Staging System for Myeloma (RISS)
...

Risk Stratification

The Medical Research Council IX trial identified 3 groups:89

  1. Favorable risk [no adverse IgH translocation, del(17p), or gain(1q)]

  2. Intermediate risk (1 adverse chromosomal abnormality),

  3. High-risk (> 1 adverse chromosomal abnormality).

  4. Ultra-high-risk was defined as >2 chromosomal aberrations (2% of patients).

Median progression-free survival (PFS) and OS of patients in the favorable-, intermediate-, and high-risk groups were 23.5, 17.8, and 11.7 months and 60.6, 41.9, and 21.7 months, respectively. Median OS in the ultra-high-risk group was 9 months.

Memorize the high risk mutations 4;14, 14;16, 17p
