Definition 

  • Neoplastic, clonal proliferation of plasma cells producing monoclonal immunoglobulin (usually M protein) resulting in endprognos-organ dysfunction
  • Usually mono-clonal and secretory

Epidemiology
**
**

  • Affects older people, median age 68y, M > F, blacks > whites
  • 2% of all cancers in US
  • Half survive at 5 years

Variant

Non-secretory Myeloma

Esp. IgD and IgE

Plasma Cell Leukemia

Pathophysiology
**
**

  • Malignant plasma cell line secretes monoclonal antibody

  • 95% produce M protein (monoclogal Ig = identical heavy chain + identical light chain; OR light chain only)

    • IgG 50%, IgA 20%, IgD 2%, IgM 0.5%)

    • 15-20% produce free light chains only

    • <5% secrete nothing

      • Oligosecretory

  • Light chains

    • Found in Serum as Kappa or Lambda light chain. Kappa > lamda has better prognosis
    • Found in Urine as Bence-Jones protein

CKD and K/L

  • The rapid turnover of FLC (2-6 hours) as compared with IgG (23 days) and other immunoglobulins (ie, Ig: 5.8 days; IgM: 5.1 days; IgD: 2.8 days; IgE: 2.3 days) contribute to FLC lability and thus make it a useful disease burden monitoring tool
  • Normally, K / L production ratio is 2:1.  K/L ratio is < 1 because kappa is a monomer and cleared more readily by kidneys. Under CKD, K/L ratio is >1 because kappa chains cannot be cleared, and both are cleared by the reticuloendothelial system

Revised Staging System for Myeloma (RISS)

Risk Stratification

The Medical Research Council IX trial identified 3 groups:89

  1. Favorable risk [no adverse IgH translocation, del(17p), or gain(1q)]

  2. Intermediate risk (1 adverse chromosomal abnormality),

  3. High-risk (> 1 adverse chromosomal abnormality).

  4. Ultra-high-risk was defined as >2 chromosomal aberrations (2% of patients).

Median progression-free survival (PFS) and OS of patients in the favorable-, intermediate-, and high-risk groups were 23.5, 17.8, and 11.7 months and 60.6, 41.9, and 21.7 months, respectively. Median OS in the ultra-high-risk group was 9 months.

Epidemiology

  • Blacks have higher prevalence of MGUS and higher progression

**Definition: **Myeloma without end organ damage (including amyloidosis) and BM plasma cells < 60%


Pathophysiology

Clonal evolution

  • Branching pattern: multiple clones that develop branching mutations
    • Slower progression
  • Stable: all the changes required for MM is already present until replication reaches threshold for myeloma
    • Shorter time to progression from MGUS to MM

Risk stratification 

Risk stratification


History of risk stratification

  • Mayo 20/20/2 score has been validated independently
  • IMWG score 2020 includes FISH cytogenetics information

20-20-20 risk for progression: 3 factor model

  • BM PC > 20%
  • M Spike > 20 g/L
  • FLC ratio > 20
  • Cytogenetics: t(4;14), t(14;16), +1q, del13q/monosomy 13
  • Risk of progression at 5 years
    • 0 risk factors: 25%,
    • 1 risk factor: 50%
    • 2+ risk factors: 75%
    • After 5 years: risk about 20% for 5-10 years; after 10 years: MGUS risk 1%/year

4-factor model


Monitoring: q2-3 months for first few years; once reach 5 years, follow q1year

10% per year risk, highest risk in first 5 years but after that they are likely more like MGUS


  • Mayo risk stratification: M spike > 20, BM >20%, FLC ratio > 20
  • 2 risk factor = high risk
    • 25% risk of progression in 2 years; 2 years median progression
    • 1/3 of all Smoldering MM: time to progression of 2 years to MM (50% risk progression at 2 years; studies support Len-dex improve risk of progression by 90%; does not impact future OS)
  • 1 risk factor = intermediate risk
  • 0 = low risk
  • High risk: 4;14; dell17p; 1q gain

IMWG scoring system 

  • The risk stratification was validated by IMWG
  • High risk = risk of progression 50%


Interval change is also important

  • For low risk patients: M spike > 0.5 g, Hgb falls by 5 —> high risk of progression

Found in low numbers 3-5% in BM

Arises in bone marrow from a B-cell precursor -> Lymph node -> antigen exposure and becomes lymphoblast -> becomes short lived plasma cell OR goes into lymph node and undergoes hypermutation and then either becomes memory cell or becomes long lived plasma cell producing antibodies (IgG, IgM, IgA, IgE)

Free Light Chain

Normal Ratio

As λ LCs are dimeric, their renal clearance is slower compared with κ LCs [Wochner et al. 1967], leading to a κ/λ ratio in the serum of about 0.58

Ratio higher in CKD

As the kidneys fail, however, the non-preferential reticulo-endothial route forms an increasing proportion of the FLC clearance

This results in a more similar serum half-life for the two FLCs and the FLC ratio therefore becomes increasingly influenced by the underlying production rates, by the plasma cells. There are approximately twice as many κ producing cells as there are λ cells and this results in a ratio of total κ to total λ in the serum of approximately 1.8

Kappa free light chains are thus higher in CKD, so ratio is 0.37-3.1 in CKD

Urine Bence Jones

Beyond a critical threshold, you pee out protein as bence jones protein

FLC serum test is pretty good, but much more expensive, compared to urinary bence jones

Uses urine test at diagnosis and work up to check for features of nephrotic syndrome, also to screen for nephrotic syndrome and establish a baseline albumin level

Quantitative Immunoglobulins

  • Tested to diagnose concomittant hypogammaglobulinemia
  • Useful for monitoring monoclonal proteins (eg. IgA) which migrate in the beta region of SPEP

Beta-2-Microglobulin

  • B2M is a LMW protein synthesized by all nucleated cells present in all body fluids
  • Shares similar structure to immunoglobulin molecules, makes beta-pleated sheets, associates with MHC-I molecules
  • Causes dialysis related amyloidosis
  • Levels correlate with tumor burden in myeloma and fall in response to therapy

POEMS: Also remember that POEMS is a very, very rare entity. In terms of monoclonal gammopathies, MGUS and multiple myeloma are by far the most common.

Polyneuropathy (often many nerves involved; characteristically progressive weakness in legs and arms): diffferential demyelinating neuropathy vs. CIDP

Organomegaly (esp HSM),
Endocrinopathy,
M-spike,

Skin abnormalities (hyperpigmentation, hypertricosis, sclerosis). Characteristically has a very high VEGF level.

Associated with high VEGF levels peripherally

  • A very rare multisystem disorder, associated with monoclonal gammopathies or plasma cell dyscrasias
  • Epi: M>F, 40-50s
  • Median time for onset of sx to dx = 13-18 mos!!
  • DDx often includes: CIDP, primary amyloidosis, cryoglobulinemia, multiple myeloma (polyneuropathy is uncommon unless also with amyloid)
  • Associatied disorders: Castleman’s disease, osteosclerotic myeloma
  • Tx: two-pronged: 1) treat the underlying plasma cell disorder, 2) treat associated abnormalities/sxs
  • Prognosis: often chronic and progressive, until bedridden. Survival is 3x longer than with multiple myeloma

Treatment:

  • cyclophosphamide and prednisone to cool off the cytokine abnormalities
  • autologous stem cell transplant

POEMS syndrome is a rare paraneoplastic disease associated with an underlying plasma cell dyscrasia and stands for polyradiculoneuropathy, organomegaly, endocrinopathy, monoclonal plasma cell disorder, and skin changes. Other important features not highlighted by this acronym include papilledema, extravascular volume overload, elevated vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF) levels, sclerotic bone lesions, erthrocytosis, and thrombocytosis. Additionally, a variant of POEMS syndrome is associated with Castleman’s disease.

The two **mandatory **major criteria for POEMS Syndrome are polyradiculoneuropathy and monoclonal plasma cell disorder. 

There are many other signs and symptoms outside of the POEMS acronym (see attached chart).

Epidemiology

Most common forms of systemic amyloidosis

AL amyloidosis may co-occur with multiple myeloma approximately 10% of the time

The plasma cell clone in AL amyloidosis is typically smaller and not as proliferative as in myeloma;

these clonal cells secrete light chains predominantly (but not exclusively) of the lambda type.

Pathophysiology


Symptoms of amyloidosis 

  • nephrotic-range proteinuria
  • heart failure with preserved ejection fraction: myocardial wall thickening with low-voltage electrocardiography waveforms
    • Tempo of onset (senile is slower)
  • a gradual decrease in the need for antihypertensive drugs
  • nondiabetic peripheral neuropathy (ascending, symmetrical with approximately 50% having antecedent carpal tunnel syndrome)
  • hepatomegaly with alkaline phosphatase elevation
  • noninfectious chronic diarrhea
  • erectile or bladder dysfunction

Case: 60 presents with fluid overload, diffuse mucocutaneoux bleeding, PT and PTT prolonged/corrects with mixing, factor X level markedly low

  • Amyloid fibrils absorb factor X leading to acquired deficiency
  • Screening: IFE, SPEP, light chain
  • Troponin, NT pro BNP for cardiac risk stratification

DIAGNOSIS

Suspect AL amyloidosis: HFpEF, nephrotic syndrome, hepatomegaly, M protein L > K, tissue diagnosis (fat pad  biopsy or organ directed biopsy). Risk stratify with troponin, proBNP.

  • Transplant eligible: ASCT
  • Non-eligible: dara+cyborD, goal dFLC < 40 (ideally < 10)

Phase III ANDROMEDA study comparing daratumumab with cyclophosphamide/bortezomib/dexamethasone to cyclo- phosphamide/bortezomib/dexamethasone
