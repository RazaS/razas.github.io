progress

There's a game called Progress Quest, which parodies traditional role-playing games by just running aimlessly in the background and playing itself. You still have 'stats', which update themselves. You get 'stronger' and more 'specialised', and a list of events take place, but you never feel any sense of accomplishment because it's only progress for its own sake. My undergraduate often felt like Progress Quest, in that I was accumulating statistics (cGPA, $ debt, course codes) that made me eligible for more quests, but that in itself usually felt empty. I can't walk into a class and get excited about the mark I could walk away with, I had to get excited about the class itself, who was sitting beside me and who was facing me. It's kind of a cliche to say that all you really take away from your university years is the people you meet. But you know, it's true. Days replace days, jobs replace jobs and degrees push earlier ones down your CV like the ticker tape newsfeed on facebook, but people don't replace people. You can transfer credits between schools, but not friends. They are all unique, your relationship to them is unique, and its the only variable that meaningfully changes between one university or another. So, honestly, it's good to have a piece of paper to show for the past 4 years but it's infinitely better to have teachers, friends and family to last well beyond. Without them, life would be just a quest for progress, but with them it feels more like a journey.
