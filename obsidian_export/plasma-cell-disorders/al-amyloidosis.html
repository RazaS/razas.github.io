Epidemiology

Most common forms of systemic amyloidosis

AL amyloidosis may co-occur with multiple myeloma approximately 10% of the time

The plasma cell clone in AL amyloidosis is typically smaller and not as proliferative as in myeloma;

these clonal cells secrete light chains predominantly (but not exclusively) of the lambda type.

Pathophysiology


Symptoms of amyloidosis 

  • nephrotic-range proteinuria
  • heart failure with preserved ejection fraction: myocardial wall thickening with low-voltage electrocardiography waveforms
    • Tempo of onset (senile is slower)
  • a gradual decrease in the need for antihypertensive drugs
  • nondiabetic peripheral neuropathy (ascending, symmetrical with approximately 50% having antecedent carpal tunnel syndrome)
  • hepatomegaly with alkaline phosphatase elevation
  • noninfectious chronic diarrhea
  • erectile or bladder dysfunction

Case: 60 presents with fluid overload, diffuse mucocutaneoux bleeding, PT and PTT prolonged/corrects with mixing, factor X level markedly low

  • Amyloid fibrils absorb factor X leading to acquired deficiency
  • Screening: IFE, SPEP, light chain
  • Troponin, NT pro BNP for cardiac risk stratification

DIAGNOSIS

Suspect AL amyloidosis: HFpEF, nephrotic syndrome, hepatomegaly, M protein L > K, tissue diagnosis (fat pad  biopsy or organ directed biopsy). Risk stratify with troponin, proBNP.

  • Transplant eligible: ASCT
  • Non-eligible: dara+cyborD, goal dFLC < 40 (ideally < 10)

Multivariate predictors for survival

RESPONSE assessment

  • dFLC: difference between involved and uninvolved free light chain
  • Previously: achieving a hematologic very good partial response was lowering the difference in free light chains to less than 40 mg/L was sufficient; now the goal has shifted to reaching a complete response (difference in free light chains < 10 mg/L)

Staging

TREATMENT

Phase III ANDROMEDA study comparing daratumumab with cyclophosphamide/bortezomib/dexamethasone to cyclo- phosphamide/bortezomib/dexamethasone

  • daratumumab resulted in a significant improvement in the overall response rate (92% vs. 77%), doubling of organ responses at 6 months (cardiac response, 42% vs. 22%; renal response, 53% vs. 24%), and a significant improvement in survival free from major organ deterioration or hematologic progression

TREATMENT ALGORITHM

  • dara-CyBorD: should response after 2 cycles, if do not, change strategy


Why transplant?

TRANSPLANT ELIGIBLE

  • Melphalan dosing based on age and organ involvement
  • If achieve CR (~33% pts) after ASCT: observation
  • If do not achieve CR: bortezomib can achieve 60% CR
  • Caveat: these are transplant eligible patients


  • D-VCd higher CR than ASCT

Transplant eligibility 

  • Transplant for those already on dialysis then amyloid
  • Conditions associated with excess mortality
    • 3 organ involvement is barrier to transplantation due to excess mortality: volume distribution becomes an issue
    • Significant cardiac involvement with elevated BNP: high risk of cardiac complications
    • Liver involvement: risk of VOD following transplant
    • GI involvement: bleeding with transplant

Comparing between induction regimens: D-VCd
