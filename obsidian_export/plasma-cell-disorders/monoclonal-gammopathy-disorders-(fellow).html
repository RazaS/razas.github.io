The Plasma Celll
Found in low numbers 3-5% in BM

Arises in bone marrow from a B-cell precursor -> Lymph node -> antigen exposure and becomes lymphoblast -> becomes short lived plasma cell OR goes into lymph node and undergoes hypermutation and then either becomes memory cell or becomes long lived plasma cell producing antibodies (IgG, IgM, IgA, IgE)

Plasma Cell Disorders

Disease Definitions

Pathophysiology

MGUS < 10% plasma cells
Myeloma >10% plasma cells

Molecular Progression

Etiology

  • IgH translocations in B-cells in germinal center

  • Cyclin D dysregulation -> clonal plasma cell proliferation

  • More mutations are accumulated such as NFkB, leading to smoldering meloma state

  • More mutations in MYC, TP53, Rb, lead to myeloma

Pathology

Histology

  • large pale blue cytoplasm
  • central migration
  • perinuclear clearing
  • multiple-nuclei in cells
  • dutcher bodies = cytoplasmic inclusions of immunoglobulins

Monoclonality

  • 1-2% of adults > 50; 4-5% adults over 70 will have monoclonal gammopathy if looked for
  • Monoclonality also presents in
    • Autoimmune disease
    • Chronic inflammation
    • MGUS
    • Neoplasms

Reactive vs. Neoplastic Proliferations

Establish clonality

  • light chain restriction
  • Immunologic abberancy (loss of normal markers, gain of abnormal markers)
  • plasma cell % (esp in biopsy rather than aspirate) -- flow not good for that

Initial Investigations
**
**

  • CBC

    • Cytopenias (anemia)
    • Rouleaux formation

  • Pre-treatment work-up

    • TB, HIV, Hep B

  • Biochemistry 

    • Increased Ca+, Cr (CRAB)
    • Quantitative immunoglobulins
    • BNP for amyloidosis
    • B2-microglobulin, albumin, LDH and CRP are poor prognosticators
    • Proteinuria
    • Urine Bence Jones Protein
    • Decreased anion gap

  • Monoclonal proteins

    • SPEP: spike in serum in 80%
    • UPEP: demonstrates light chains in urine (15% only have this)
      • UPEP and Urine Immunofixation – 24 hour urine collecton – similar concepts as above.   Main reason to do this is that it picks up many patients who have light chain secretion only, as those are rapidly excreted in the urine and thus are often not picked up on SPEP.
    • Serum Immunofixation – always done with SPEP, uses monospecific Abs for heavy and light chains – increases sensitivity to pick up M-protein and determines clonality (monoclonal vs polyclonal). Unlike SPEP, does not give quantitative estimate.
    • Serum free light chain quantification: kappa and lambda

  • Bone marrow aspirate and biopsy

    • 10% plasma cells with abnormal morphology

    • Send for FISH or cytogenetics

  • Skeletal series (CT > MRI > X-Ray)

    • Lytic lesions, sclerotic lesions
    • Bone scans not useful since they detect osteoblast activity

Electrophoresis
**

Immunoglobulins in myeloma...

  • Can be Heavy chain (IgG >  IgA > IgD > IgM 0.5%)
  • Can be Light chain (kappa, lambda)
    • The rapid turnover of FLC (2-6 hours) as compared with IgG (23 days) and other immunoglobulins (ie, Ig: 5.8 days; IgM: 5.1 days; IgD: 2.8 days; IgE: 2.3 days) contribute to FLC lability and thus make it a useful disease burden monitoring tool
  • Can be Heavy + light chain (excess light chains)
  • Can be Light chain only
  • Can be heavy chain only
  • Can secrete nothing..! <5%

Pearls

  • Electrophoresis can distinguish large M-peaks (monoclonal protein)
  • Some M-proteins can hide under other bands. E.g. IgD can often migrate with Beta instead of Gamma, and needs to be followed by IFE instead and Immunoglobulin levels
  • Free light chains can be followed in serum, or in urine via bence jones protein

Free Light Chain

Normal Ratio

As λ LCs are dimeric, their renal clearance is slower compared with κ LCs [Wochner et al. 1967], leading to a κ/λ ratio in the serum of about 0.58

Ratio higher in CKD

As the kidneys fail, however, the non-preferential reticulo-endothial route forms an increasing proportion of the FLC clearance

This results in a more similar serum half-life for the two FLCs and the FLC ratio therefore becomes increasingly influenced by the underlying production rates, by the plasma cells. There are approximately twice as many κ producing cells as there are λ cells and this results in a ratio of total κ to total λ in the serum of approximately 1.8

Kappa free light chains are thus higher in CKD, so ratio is 0.37-3.1 in CKD

Urine Bence Jones

Beyond a critical threshold, you pee out protein as bence jones protein

FLC serum test is pretty good, but much more expensive, compared to urinary bence jones

Uses urine test at diagnosis and work up to check for features of nephrotic syndrome, also to screen for nephrotic syndrome and establish a baseline albumin level

Quantitative Immunoglobulins

  • Tested to diagnose concomittant hypogammaglobulinemia
  • Useful for monitoring monoclonal proteins (eg. IgA) which migrate in the beta region of SPEP

Beta-2-Microglobulin

  • B2M is a LMW protein synthesized by all nucleated cells present in all body fluids
  • Shares similar structure to immunoglobulin molecules, makes beta-pleated sheets, associates with MHC-I molecules
  • Causes dialysis related amyloidosis
  • Levels correlate with tumor burden in myeloma and fall in response to therapy
